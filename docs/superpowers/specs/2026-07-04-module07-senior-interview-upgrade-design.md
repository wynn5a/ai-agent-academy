## Per-lesson changes

1. **01 the-eval-pyramid** — model/canon already clean; added "Eval economics: matching cost to cadence" (unit cost by orders of magnitude per tier, cost/frequency/fidelity trade, cadence table) and "Capability evals vs. regression evals" (are-we-getting-better vs. did-we-break-something, why blending them into one number hides regressions); predict exercise (moving the judge tier off the PR gate entirely trades detection latency for cost — fix is splitting the tier by risk, not just changing cadence); drills (design the pyramid for a PR-opening coding agent; flat regression score vs. "quality feels worse" — suite staleness). 28 → 38 min.
2. **02 llm-as-judge** — fixed stale `claude-sonnet-4-5` → `claude-sonnet-5`; added "Rubric design: the real work of judge quality" (decomposition over holistic scales, anchored pass/fail examples, rubric as versioned artifact) with a decomposed-vs-holistic rubric code example, and "Judge ensembles: when they earn their cost" (linear cost scaling, worth it at high-stakes single verdicts, not worth it for aggregate trend tracking — ties back to Lesson 3's statistical-rigor argument); spot-the-bug (vague 1–10 rubric at 61% agreement — fix the rubric, not the model); drills (build an empathy judge end to end; when to spend on an ensemble, and what a 2-1 split means). 30 → 40 min.
3. **03 regression-suites-in-ci** — fixed a canon violation: the "run at temperature 0 to fight flakiness" callout and keypoint (current frontier Claude models reject temperature/top_p/top_k with a 400, so this advice was actively wrong) rewritten to explain why that lever doesn't exist and what to do instead; added "Statistical rigor at small n" (why a 2-point delta on 50 items is noise, confidence-interval magnitude, pass@k vs. pass@1, paired comparisons beating independent aggregates) with a Wilson-interval + paired-sign-test code example, and "CI integration realities" (caching model outputs by input hash, hard cost ceilings, predefined override policy and who can override); spot-the-bug turning the temperature-0 anti-pattern itself into a teaching moment (400 error, and the real fix); drills (79%→81% delta — ship or not; designing an override policy that doesn't get used weekly). 26 → 36 min.
4. **04 tracing-and-cost** — fixed two stale `claude-sonnet-4-5` → `claude-sonnet-5`; added "Online monitoring vs. offline evals" (offline suites answer "safe on anticipated inputs," online monitoring answers "drifting on inputs we didn't anticipate") covering canary sets, drift metrics, and user-feedback signals as weak/selection-biased labels; predict exercise (flat thumbs-down rate masking tripled ticket volume — convenience-sample selection bias); drills (defending production monitoring against a stable 95% offline suite; designing the full traced/dashboarded/paged observability stack with alert tiering). 27 → 37 min.
5. **05 injection-hitl-postmortems** — added "Direct vs. indirect injection" (attacker-is-user vs. attacker-plants-in-third-party-content-the-victim-never-sees) and "Why 'just prompt it not to' fails" (system-prompt instructions have no mechanical enforcement, just more competing context) ahead of the existing input-filtering section; added "Tool gating on tainted context" as a new defense-in-depth layer with a taint-tracking code example (the layer that doesn't depend on the model resisting anything); added "What's different from a classic ops postmortem" (non-determinism means no clean repro guarantee; the trace, not a stack trace, is the only witness); spot-the-bug (a "never follow embedded instructions" system-prompt line defeated by indirect injection — needs structural backup); drills (lethal trifecta for a GitHub-browsing coding agent; writing a blameless root-cause paragraph and explaining what makes it blameless). 30 → 40 min.
