## Findings (most severe first)

### [Major] "The limits are the same" is no longer true for OpenAI structured outputs
- **Where:** `content/modules/module01/lessons/04-structured-outputs.ts:88` (OpenAI variant explanation: _"the constrained-decoding guarantees and limits are the same"_); reinforced by the provider-neutral absolutism at `:159–163` (_"numeric ranges, string lengths, and recursive schemas are not enforced by constrained decoding"_), `:168` (_"SDK helpers … quietly strip unsupported constraints"_), and the spot-the-bug answer at `:230` (_"those constraints are either rejected or silently stripped before decoding"_).
- **Standard:** 1 (factual accuracy) and 4 (Anthropic + OpenAI) — the same sentence fails both.
- **Issue:** The lesson teaches, as a universal property of constrained decoding, that `minimum`/`maximum`/`minLength`/`maxLength` cannot be enforced and that both providers share this limit. That is **verified true for Anthropic** — its current structured-outputs docs list numeric and string-length constraints as *not supported* (the SDK strips them and validates client-side). But **OpenAI diverged in 2025**: Structured Outputs now accepts and documents `minLength`, `maxLength`, `pattern`, `format`, `minimum`, `maximum`, `multipleOf`, `minItems`, and `maxItems` as *supported* schema keywords (announced with the "many more keywords are now supported" update). So OpenAI does **not** reject or strip them, and "the limits are the same" is contradicted. (Nuance to preserve: OpenAI's support is documented but enforcement in practice isn't guaranteed — OpenAI itself still recommends validating after parsing, so the lesson's *operational* advice survives; only the equivalence/absolutism is wrong.)
- **Why it matters:** This sits exactly where the user is most sensitive — accuracy *and* correct provider representation. A senior candidate who says in an OpenAI-flavored interview "structured outputs can't enforce numeric ranges" will be corrected on a feature OpenAI shipped in 2025; that reads as "learned this from a 2024 blog post." The lesson currently trains the wrong instinct for one of the two providers it exists to teach.
- **Suggested fix:** Rescope the equivalence, don't delete the teaching. At `:88`, replace "the constrained-decoding guarantees and limits are the same" with something like: _"the guarantee is the same, but the limits differ: Anthropic can't enforce value-level constraints (`minimum`/`maxLength`) and strips them; OpenAI added support for these keywords in 2025, so it accepts them — though enforcement still isn't guaranteed, so validate regardless."_ At `:159–163` scope the mechanism claim to "pure grammar-only constrained decoding (e.g. Anthropic today)" and add one sentence that OpenAI extends beyond the pure-grammar limit for value constraints. The `severity: 9` spot-the-bug (`:230`) stays valid but its "rejected or silently stripped" clause should be scoped to the Anthropic/`parse()` path it's actually describing. Keep the punchline — "'Guaranteed structure' never meant 'guaranteed values'" — it's still correct and still senior.

### [Minor] The constrained-decoding mechanism has no diagram, where one would carry it
- **Where:** `content/modules/module01/lessons/04-structured-outputs.ts:154–163` ("How constrained decoding actually works").
- **Standard:** 6 (tables/diagrams/animations — flagging an *absence*).
- **Issue:** Logit-masking at each decode step is an inherently visual, per-step process — it's the same family of mechanism the exemplar (lesson 02) chose to animate with `token-selection`. Here it's prose-only. The prose is good, but "the logits of all tokens that would violate the grammar are masked out (set to −∞)" is precisely the kind of step-wise transform that a small animation (candidate logits → grammar mask → survivors → sample) makes graspable at a glance.
- **Why it matters:** This is the "why" interviewers dig for (the lesson says so at `:159`). A diagram would raise retention of the one idea the lesson most wants a learner to be able to whiteboard. Lower priority only because it requires a new `AnimationName`/component, not a content-only edit.
- **Suggested fix:** Consider a new animation (e.g. `constrained-decoding`) showing the mask step; or, cheaper, a small `code`/`text` block that lays out one decode step visually (candidate tokens with two struck through by the grammar). Not a blocker — the lesson is comprehensible without it.
